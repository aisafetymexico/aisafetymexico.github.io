## 0 · Working principle (summary of `docs/RAND-AESTHETIC.md`)

> RAND looks institutional because **typography + whitespace + hairlines** carry
> all the hierarchy. They use:
>  - 95% **white** surfaces. One warm-grey band (newsletter / closing block).
>  - **No card shadows**. No hover lifts. No edge-accent gradients. No
>    decorative underline pills under H2s. No image drop shadows.
>  - **Heavy bold sans** H1 (~64–72 px on desktop) sitting on white, no eyebrow,
>    no chip — only a tiny grey breadcrumb above.
>  - Cards are **photo + small uppercase label + bold title + date**, separated
>    by hairline rules — no fills, no shadows.
>  - Single warm-grey band wraps "Subscribe to Newsletter" — that's the **only**
>    coloured surface besides photos.
>
> Our site reads marketing-y because we replicate the opposite of every one of
> those rules: navy band heroes, alternating cream/white zebra, copper-gold
> gradient ornaments under every H2, drop-shadowed cards on every grid, pill
> badges, gradient buttons with shadow-cta, and inset section boxshadows.
